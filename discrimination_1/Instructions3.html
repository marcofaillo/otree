INSTRUCTIONS 3

Ranking and random matching

Finally, you will be asked to rank the characteristics on a scale from 1 to 5, where 1 indicates the most important for you.

After you have made your ranking, we will randomly choose one of the five characteristics and we will randomly match you with another co-participant.

The probability that a decision based on that characteristic will be implemented depends on your ranking. See the table below.

Rank Probability
First 100%
Second 90%
Third 80%
Fourth 70%
Fifth 60%
 

If the characteristic you ranked first is chosen, your revised decision based on the types in that characteristic will be implemented with 100% probability.

If the second characteristic is chosen, your revised decision based on the types in that characteristic will be implemented with a 90% probability. With a 10% probability, your round 1 decision will be implemented, disregarding your co-participant’s type.

The same applies to subsequent rankings.

So if you are matched with a person who was born on an odd calendar day and you decided to revise you first round choice and to send X points to people with that characteristic, then if you ranked this characteristic as first, that person will receive x points for sure. If instead you have ranked it third, that that person will receive X points with 60% of probability and the points you decided to send in the first round with a probability of 40%.
