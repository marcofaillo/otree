INSTRUCTIONS 4

Stage 3: Second actual choice

In Stage 3, you may see some additional information about your co-participants, depending on your position in the network.

First, you may see what they chose to send you in Stage 2, and if this corresponds to their stated intentions of Stage 1.

Second, you may receive some information about how your co-participants have interacted with each other. This includes

  • Their stated intentions in Stage 1, and
  • their actual decisions in Stage 2.

The information available to you and your co-participants depends on your and their position in the network. For example, consider the network in Figure 3.

   
Figure 3
   

You are A, so you are connected to B and C.

  • You observe the Stage 2 choices of both B and C (dashed arrows with the eye icon). So, you will know if both B and C have confirmed their stated intention of Stage 1.
  • You also receive information from B about C’s behavior toward B in both Stage 1 and Stage 2 (solid arrow with the envelope icon). This means that you will know if C has confirmed her stated intention in the interaction with B.
  • Finally, you receive information from C about B’s behavior toward C in both Stage 1 and Stage 2 (solid arrow with the envelope icon). This means that you will know if B has confirmed her stated intention in the interaction with C.

B and C neither observe the other’s choices nor receive additional information.

After observing this information, all players make a final decision about the points to send to each the two co-participants.

This decision cannot be changed.

This is the end of the game.
