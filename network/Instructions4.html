INSTRUCTIONS 4

Stage 3: Second actual choice

In Stage 3, depending on your position in the network, you may see some additional information about your co-participants.

First, you may see what they chose to send you in Stage 2, and if this corresponds with their intentions stated in Stage 1.

Second, you may receive some information about how your co-participants have interacted with each other. This includes

  • their stated intentions in Stage 1, and
  • their actual decisions in Stage 2.

The information available to you and your co-participants depends on your and their position in the network. For example, consider the network in Figure 3.

   
Figure 3
   

You are A, so you are connected to B and C.

  • You observe the Stage 2 choices of both B and C (expressed by the dashed arrows with the eye icon in Figure 3). This means that you will know if both B and C have confirmed their stated intention from Stage 1. So, you will know if both B and C have confirmed their stated intention of Stage 1.
  • You also receive information from B about C's behavior towards B in Stages 1 and 2 (expressed by a solid arrow with an envelope icon). This means that you will know if C has confirmed their stated intention in their interaction with B.
  • Finally, you receive information from C about B’s behavior towards C in both Stages 1 and 2 (expressed by a solid arrow with the envelope icon). This means that you will know if B has confirmed their stated intention in their interaction with C.

Neither B nor C observe the other’s choices nor receive additional information.

After receiving this role-dependent information, all players make a final decision about the points to send to eachto each of the two co-participants.

This decision cannot be changed.

This is the end of the game.
