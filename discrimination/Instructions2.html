INSTRUCTIONS 2

Round 2

If you decide to move to round 2, you will be asked to think about the person with whom you could be matched. In particular, you will see a possible description of what type of co-participant you could be matched, based on his/her answers to the same short survey that you have filled in at the beginning of the experiment.

At this point, for each type of co-participant, you can decide to confirm or revise your round 1 choice.

For example, let’s consider a characteristic that will not be used in this activity: your co-participant’s height , as shown in Figure 2.

As you can see at the top of the screen, you are reminded of your first-round choice. Then, there is a short description of the type of co-participant you could be matched with, based on that characteristic.

For each type, you have to decide whether to confirm your first-round choice or to change it.

       

Finally you will be asked to rank them on a scale from 1 to 5, where 1 is the most important characteristic for you.

After you have made your ranking, we will randomly choose one of the five characteristics to match you with a specific co-participant.

The probability that a revised decision based on the types in one characteristic will be choosen depends on your ranking. See table below

Rank Probability
First 100%
Second 90%
Third 80%
Fourth 70%
 

Then if the category you ranked first is chosen, your revised decision based on the types in that category will be implemented with 100% probability;

if the category you ranked second is chosen, your revised decision based on the types in that category will be implemented with 90% probability, and with 10% probability your first-round decision will be implemented, which disregards your co-participant’s type;

And so on.
