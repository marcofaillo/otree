INSTRUCTIONS 2

Round 2

If you decide to move to round 2, you will be asked to think about the person with whom you could be. In particular, you will see a possible description of what type of co-participant you could be matched, based on his/her answers to the same short survey that you have filled in at the beginning of the experiment.

At this point you can decide to confirm your round 1 choice, applying it independently of the type of co-participant, or you can change it in case your co-participant has some specific characteristic.

For example, let’s consider a characteristic that will not be used in this activity: your co-participant’s height , as shown in Figure 2.

As you can see at the top of the screen, you are reminded of your first-round choice. Then, there is a short description of the characteristic you are receiving and the different types of co-participants (tall, medium-sized, or short) with whom you could be matched.

For each type, you have to decide whether to confirm your first-round choice or to change it.

       

Once you have concluded ... rounds, we will ask you to rank the ... categories for which you have been given the opportunity to revise your first-round decision. Please rank them on a scale from 1 to 5, where 1 is the most important category for you. After you have made your ranking, we will randomly choose one of the five categories to match you with a specific co-participant. Once you have concluded <6> rounds, we will ask you to rank the <5> categories for which you have been given the opportunity to revise your first-round decision.

Please rank them on a scale from 1 to 5, where 1 is the most important category for you.

After you have made your ranking, we will randomly choose one of the five categories to match you with a specific co-participant.

The probability that a revised decision based on the types in one category will be choosen depends on your ranking. See table below

Rank Probability
First 100%
Second 90%
Third 80%
Fourth 70%
 

Then if the category you ranked first is chosen, your revised decision based on the types in that category will be implemented with 100% probability;

if the category you ranked second is chosen, your revised decision based on the types in that category will be implemented with 90% probability, and with 10% probability your first-round decision will be implemented, which disregards your co-participant’s type;

And so on.
