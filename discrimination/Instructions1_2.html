INSTRUCTIONS 1

In this activity, you will be paired with another person who is randomly chosen and asked to make the same decisions as you. We call this person your “co-participant”.

The activity is divided into 6 rounds. Over the next pages, we provide you with a full description of what you will be asked to do in each round.

 

The activity: round 1

In round 1, you will receive 10 points. You must then decide how many points to send to your co-participant. You will keep the remaining points.

Each point you send will be doubled and delivered to your co-participant.

Your co-participant will make the same decision (see Figure 1).

     

At the end of the activity, each point will be converted at a rate of 1 point = 0.10£.

The total number of points you will earn is equal to:

Points Earned = Your Initial 10 Points – The Points You Sent + ( 2×The Points your Co-Participant Sent)

For example, if you send 2 points to your co-participant and they send you 3 points, the total number of points you earn is

10-2+(2×3)=14

and your co-participant earns:

10-3+(2×2)=11

If, instead, you send 3 points to your co-participant and they send you 4 points, the total number of points you earn is:

10-3+(2×4)=15

and your co-participant earns:

10-4+(2×3)=12

This is the end of round 1.

At this point you can either choose to stop and move to a questionnaire, or you can stay for round 2.

If you stay, you have the opportunity to update your choice based on information about the characteristics of your co-participant.

After receiving that information, you can revise your first-round decision depending on the characteristics of your co-participant.
